A spring balances gravity, then rings down to rest

Add a spring along the slope. At its rest point the spring's squeeze exactly carries the down-slope pull of gravity. Push the block away from there and release: the leftover force drives an oscillation while damping bleeds energy until it settles.

At equilibrium the acceleration is zero, so kx_eq = mg sin θ, giving x_eq = mg sin θ ⁄ k. Displace the block from there and Newton's law above governs the return.

The damping ratio ζ = b ⁄ 2√(km) decides the character: ζ < 1 overshoots and rings (under-damped), ζ = 1 is the fastest no-overshoot return (critical), ζ > 1 crawls back (over-damped). Static friction also adds a small dead-band where the block can stop short.
