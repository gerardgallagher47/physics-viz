Divergence (Gauss) Theorem · Conservation of Mass

What leaves a closed surface equals what's produced inside it.

For a steady flow, no mass accumulates inside the control volume, so the mass crossing the inlet must equal the mass crossing the outlet. Walls carry no flux because the velocity runs parallel to them.

Shrink the control volume to a point and the surface integral becomes the differential continuity equation — the equation a CFD solver enforces in every cell.
