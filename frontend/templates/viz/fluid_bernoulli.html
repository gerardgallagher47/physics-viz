Pressure, speed and height share one energy budget

Follow a parcel of fluid along a streamline. Its energy comes in three forms — pressure, motion, and height. Bernoulli's principle says their sum stays fixed, so if the flow speeds up or rises, the pressure must drop to pay for it.

Each term is an energy per unit volume (units of pressure, Pa). Between any two points on the same streamline, P₁ + ½ρv₁² + ρgh₁ = P₂ + ½ρv₂² + ρgh₂.

It holds for flow that is steady, incompressible and inviscid (no friction). Continuity, A₁v₁ = A₂v₂, sets how much the speed term grows when the pipe narrows — explored in the next lesson.
