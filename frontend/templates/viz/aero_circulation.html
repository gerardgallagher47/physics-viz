Kutta–Joukowski Theorem

Lift is circulation times free-stream momentum.

A wing acts like a bound vortex of strength Γ. Superposed on the free stream, it speeds the air over the top and slows it underneath — low pressure above, high pressure below, and a net upward force.

With no circulation there is no lift at all (d'Alembert's paradox). The sharp trailing edge and the Kutta condition are what set Γ for a real airfoil.
