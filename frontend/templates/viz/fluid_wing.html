How a pressure difference lifts a wing

Air travels faster over the curved top of the airfoil than under the flatter bottom. By Bernoulli, faster flow means lower pressure — so the top surface is suction and the bottom is push. Summed over the wing, that difference is lift.

Both lift and drag scale with the dynamic pressure q = ½ρV², so doubling the speed roughly quadruples the lift. C_L and C_D are dimensionless coefficients set by the airfoil shape and angle of attack, and c is the chord length.

Their ratio L/D = C_L ⁄ C_D is the wing's aerodynamic efficiency — fixed by geometry, not by how fast you fly.
